Gloomhaven Universe

Why are we here ....

Some say this site was stitched together by candlelight and caffeine. Others claim it appeared one night, summoned by a bug-sized bard with a knack for naming scrolls.
Truth is—this is a portal.
A place where adventurers of all ages gather to explore the realms of Buttons and Bugs, Gloomhaven, Frosthaven, and the digital echoes of Gloomhaven Online.
It's where beginners book their first lessons, parties form in the shadows, and meetups spark new legends.
Whether you're here to learn, play, or simply wander through the codex, you've found the right scroll. The journey begins with curiosity—and ends wherever you decide to draw the map.

But what are these games? ....

It's not a video game. It's not a quiz. It's not a roll-the-dice-and-hope affair.
This is a tabletop campaign game—a genre where players gather around a table to explore maps, battle creatures, and shape a shared story. Think of it as part board game (with pieces, cards, and maps) and part roleplaying game (you become a character, grow stronger, and make choices that matter).
But unlike classic board games, there's no race to the finish. And unlike traditional RPGs, there's no game master or improv acting (although welcome at all times).
Instead:
- You play cards to move, attack, and use abilities—no dice involved.
- You plan each turn carefully, managing your hand like a spellbook.
- You unlock new content as you go—sealed envelopes, new characters, evolving maps. It's strategic.
It's story-driven. It's a slow-burning saga told in cardboard and courage.

Buttons and Bugs

Game Description

Not every hero starts at full-size. Some get shrunken. Others do the shrinking.
Buttons & Bugs is a pocket-sized adventure where strategy meets whimsy. Designed for quick quests and replayable delight, it shrinks epic decision-making into a world where every move matters—and every bug might bite back.
Perfect for newcomers and seasoned tacticians alike, this game invites you to: - Outsmart your opponent in bite-sized battles - Explore a world stitched from scraps and secrets
Play anywhere—on a train, at a tavern, or beneath a blanket fort It's cozy. It's clever. And it's crawling with charm.

Gloomhaven

Game Description

Gold talks. Steel listens. And somewhere in the fog, fate takes notes.
Gloomhaven is a sprawling tactical campaign where mercenaries chase coin, clash with monsters, and accidentally become legends.
You'll crawl through crypts, bargain with demons, and retire just in time to open a bakery—or burn down a temple. No dice. Just decisions. No heroes. Just hired hands with questionable morals and excellent card decks.
Every choice reshapes the world. Every battle is a puzzle. And every unlocked character is a new flavor of chaos. Welcome to Gloomhaven. Wipe your boots. The city bites.

Frosthaven

Game Description

The ice doesn't care who you were. Only who you become.
Frosthaven is a standalone sequel to Gloomhaven, where mercenaries trade city streets for snowdrifts, and gold for survival.
It's colder, harsher, and somehow even more ambitious.
You'll build a settlement from scratch, fend off invasions, and unravel mysteries buried beneath the permafrost. The monsters are meaner. The choices matter more and the cold has a way of sharpening your tactics—and your regrets.
New classes. New enemies. New ways to fail gloriously. This isn't just a dungeon crawl. It's a frostbitten saga of grit, growth, and glorious loot. Bundle up. The north remembers.

United States 5*

I have never seen such an intricate, beautifully designed game. We'd finished playing Gloomhaven; Jaws of the Lion, and I expected this to simply be a continuation of that game (which I also highly recommend, especially before playing Frosthaven) - but it's so much more than that! Really complex play + varied scenarios + well made game pieces. We play as a family - our precocious 13 year old is obsessed with it, but it's too complex for our 8 year old to play as an independent character. However, the modular and scenario-based format means that she can bring a character into the game when she's ready. We have it set up on a large game table basically permanently, since we enjoy it so much; however, you can repack it if that's not feasible. We also play in short bursts - say, a round or two before bed. You don't have to complete the scenarios each time if you can keep the set up in place. Be warned: we had to upgrade our game table size for this - with our set-up, we easily need 9+ sq. ft (current table is 36 x 42 and it still feels cramped!)

Gloomhaven Online

Game Description

Same monsters. Same mercenaries. Now with less cardboard and more bandwidth.
Gloomhaven Online lets you dive into the dungeon from your desktop—no setup, no sorting, just tactical glory on demand.
Host a game, sling invites, and sync your saves like a true digital dungeonmaster.
It's the full campaign, re-coded for clicks: - Card-driven combat, no dice required - Online co-op for up to four mercs - Cross-platform chaos and cloud-saved regrets.
Whether you're soloing in silence or scheming with friends across time zones, the city still bites. Now it just loads faster.

Italy 2*

Gloomhaven tries to bring the experience of the board game on our screens, but fails to recreate the feel of being around a table. The game, although very loyal to the source, lacks pacing and the missions (very repetitive after a while), even at the lowest levels, are incredibly hard and for the most part frustrating. It is definitely overwhelming for a new player, and way too much relying on RNG for a good result. A good opportunity that sadly doesn't really deliver.
